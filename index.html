Dealing with a Pile of Problems

Life is a great gift that is granted from above. However, sometimes this gift is burdened with stress, doubt, and suffering. Sooner or later, it often happens that problems all arrive at once—academic failures, financial struggles, family troubles, and personal disappointments. You may feel like you are drowning and you cannot find a way out.

You are not the only person to have something like this. All people have phases when hardships accumulate. It is the kind of reminder that I had in this blog—there is a way through. This is how you can handle it one step at a time.

1. Focus on One Problem at a Time

We sometimes deal with multiple problems at once, which overwhelm us and prevent us from doing something. When faced with many problems, you should not get overwhelmed by trying to solve them all at once. The best thing to do is deal with the most pressing or controllable problem. You will have more energy to devote to something more important once you begin to address the biggest problem that consumes most of your energy. When you have solved one problem, you obtain the momentum and the clear mind.

For instance, a student with academic failure, part-time job conflict, and personal stress can start with academic performance first. They are able to recover little by little by simply establishing a study schedule or finding a tutor and thus gain control in that aspect, and it gets to be easier to take care of other issues next.

2. Know That Everything Is Temporary

2 Corinthians 4:17 says that, "For our present troubles are small and won’t last very long. Yet they produce for us a glory that vastly outweighs them and will last forever!" There is a tendency to think that even painful situations will never end; however, they do. Although they might be heavy, problems are temporary. Situations dissolve with time, and recovery is also possible. Probably, you are thinking of a person who has experienced a breakup, a failed project, or an unexpected job loss. It will be accompanied by the feeling that the world is ending in the moment. However, with time, when the opportunities come up, when the spirit shifts, or when healing resources are available, they tend to understand that an end was only a new beginning.

3. Remember All the Bigger Problems You Encountered and Overcame

We all have gone through really tough times before. Considering such moments will make you gain confidence and reduce fear. Just imagine that somebody failed an important exam and then applied himself more seriously and passed the exam on the second trial. Or a person who feels at one time rejected or isolated, yet later on, he/she has found supportive relationships. These historical successes show you can survive and can survive challenges many more times.

4. Share Your Problems with an Acquaintance

You do not have to carry the heavy load of problems by yourself. Discussing your problem with a person—your friend, classmate, work colleague, or counselor—will reduce your burden. An example is when an individual facing financial or emotional turmoil talks to a peer, and this only requires telling the person what we are experiencing, which would bring about some form of relief. This, in most instances, results in unforeseen guidance, encouragement, or even the simple experience of realizing that someone is concerned. Admitting to yourself, your loved ones, friends, or even a therapist that you're not okay is the first step towards facing your difficulties. You must talk about your feelings and validate yourself.

5. Pray

There will always be times when the problems come to the point that dealing with them can only be reached through faith. The attitude of asking for answers through prayer is not only important, but also seeking an avenue of peace and direction. Most of the individuals resort to praying when they are helpless. When they say their words or keep quiet in those quiet moments, they feel that they are heard, comforted, and strengthened. Whether it is a religious or philosophical take on life, slowing down to contemplate and abandon the situation might allow a person to find clarity and peace that are not to be found in the world.

Life can make us experience things we are not quite particularly prepared to deal with. Faith, support, patience, and good character can help clear the biggest heap of problems, one step at a time. In case you are reading this blog with a weary heart, remind yourself that it is not just you alone. This season will run. And your story is not yet done. It is still being written—beautifully, one chapter at a time.
